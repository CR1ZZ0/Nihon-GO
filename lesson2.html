Japanese Counting


Japanese Numbers from 1 to 1,000,000


Introduction

Learning how to count in Japanese is one of the most useful and important parts of the language. Whether you're shopping, telling the time, or talking about dates or money, numbers come up all the time. Japanese numbers follow clear patterns and can be easy to learn once you understand how they are grouped and pronounced. This guide will help you count from 1 all the way to 1,000,000 in Japanese.

Note: "shi" (4) and "shichi" (7) are sometimes avoided because they sound like words related to death or suffering.

Numbers 11 to 99

Japanese numbers between 11 and 99 follow a simple pattern:

  • 11 = juu ichi
  • 21 = ni juu ichi
  • 45 = yon juu go
  • 99 = kyuu juu kyuu

The pattern is: [tens digit] + juu + [ones digit]

Ten-Thousands and Up (10,000–1,000,000)

Japanese groups large numbers by ten-thousands instead of thousands. The word for 10,000 is man (万).

  • 10,000 = 一万 = ichi man
  • 20,000 = 二万 = ni man
  • 100,000 = 十万 = juu man
  • 1,000,000 = 百万 = hyaku man

Examples:

  • 12,000 = 一万二千 = ichi man ni sen
  • 25,000 = 二万五千 = ni man go sen
  • 88,800 = 八万八千八百 = hachi man hassen happyaku
  • 123,456 = 十二万三千四百五十六 = juu ni man san zen yon hyaku go juu roku
Remember: 1万 = 10,000, 10万 = 100,000, 100万 = 1,000,000
