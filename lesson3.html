Japanese Phrases

Lesson 3: Basic Greetings and Introductions

1. Common Greetings

In Japanese, greetings are essential for social interaction. These phrases are used daily and help form the foundation for casual and formal conversations.

Japanese Romanization English
こんにちは Konnichiwa Hello / Good afternoon
おはようございます Ohayou gozaimasu Good morning
こんばんは Konbanwa Good evening
おやすみなさい Oyasuminasai Good night
ありがとう Arigatou Thank you
すみません Sumimasen Excuse me / I'm sorry
はい Hai Yes
いいえ Iie No

2. Introductions

When introducing yourself or meeting someone for the first time, it's important to use polite phrases. Here are some useful expressions:

Japanese Romanization English
はじめまして Hajimemashite Nice to meet you
私の名前は [Name] です Watashi no namae wa [Name] desu My name is [Name]
よろしくお願いします Yoroshiku onegaishimasu Please take care of it / I look forward to working with you
[Name] と申します [Name] to moushimasu I am called [Name]

3. Asking How Someone Is

In Japanese, asking about someone's well-being is a common way to show interest in others. Here are some phrases:

Japanese Romanization English
お元気ですか? Ogenki desu ka? How are you?
元気です Genki desu I'm fine / I'm good
はい、元気です Hai, genki desu Yes, I'm fine
少し疲れています Sukoshi tsukarete imasu I'm a little tired
あまり元気ではありません Amari genki de wa arimasen I'm not feeling very well

4. Age-Related Expressions

When introducing yourself, it's also common to mention your age. In Japan, it's polite to use age in context, especially in formal conversations. Here are some expressions:

Japanese Romanization English
私は [Age] 歳です Watashi wa [Age] sai desu I am [Age] years old
何歳ですか? Nan sai desu ka? How old are you?
[Age] 歳です [Age] sai desu I am [Age] years old

Example Sentences:

  • 私の名前はジョンです。私は２０歳です。
    *Watashi no namae wa Jon desu. Watashi wa 20 sai desu.*
    "My name is John. I am 20 years old."
  • 私は田中です。何歳ですか?
    *Watashi wa Tanaka desu. Nan sai desu ka?*
    "I am Tanaka. How old are you?"

5. Saying Goodbye

When you leave or say goodbye, you can use the following phrases. It's polite to use these when ending a conversation or leaving a place:

Japanese Romanization English
さようなら Sayounara Goodbye
じゃね Ja ne See you
またね Mata ne See you later
失礼します Shitsurei shimasu Excuse me (when leaving)
お疲れ様でした Otsukaresama deshita Good work (used for coworkers)

6. Practice Dialogues

Dialogue 1: Basic Introduction

A: はじめまして、私はタナカです。
Hajimemashite, watashi wa Tanaka desu.
"Nice to meet you, I'm Tanaka."

B: はじめまして、ジョンです。
Hajimemashite, Jon desu.
"Nice to meet you, I'm John."

A: どうぞよろしくお願いします。
Douzo yoroshiku onegaishimasu.
"Please take care of me."

B: よろしくお願いします。
Yoroshiku onegaishimasu.
"Please take care of me too."

Dialogue 2: Asking How Someone Is

A: お元気ですか?
Ogenki desu ka?
"How are you?"

B: はい、元気です。あなたは?
Hai, genki desu. Anata wa?
"Yes, I'm fine. How about you?"

A: 元気です。
Genki desu.
"I'm fine."

Dialogue 3: Saying Goodbye

A: じゃね!
Ja ne!
"See you!"

B: またね!
Mata ne!
"See you later!"
